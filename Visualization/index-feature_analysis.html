Overall Findings of the Feature Analysis

Most features have an impact attrition, but a few are more consistent

Looking at the overall employee dataset, almost all features have some impact on attrition. Commonly, however, MonthlyIncome, Age, TotalWorkingYears, and YearsAtCompany are the ones that are typically in the 'top 5' in our assessments.

Feature importance to attrition does vary based on job category

The Tech job category is more sensitive to Age, TotalWorkingYears, YearsInCurrentRole; and less to YearsWithCurrentMaanger, and JobRole.

The NonTech job category is more sensitive to StockOptionLevel, NumberOfCompaniesWorkedFor, DistanceFromHome; and less to YearsWithCurrManager.

The Leadership job category is more sensitive to YearsSinceLastPromotion, Education, JobLevel, RelationshipSatisfaction, PerformanceRating; and less to Age, MonthlyIncome. Of note, the fact that leadership is less sensitive to MonthlyIncome may be a reflection of the fact that they already tend to make more, as seen from distribution analysis, and therefore it is not as much of a consideration for them as they're already paid higher than most.
